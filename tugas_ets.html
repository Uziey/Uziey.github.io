About Steam

Steam is the world's largest digital distribution platform for PC gaming, developed by Valve Corporation and launched in 2003. It acts as a massive online store, community hub, and library manager, allowing users to buy, play, and automatically update over 30,000 games across Windows, Mac, and Linux.

  • Controller Support: Deep integration with various controllers, including PlayStation, Xbox, and Nintendo, with customizable layouts.
  • Cross-Platform: Available on PC, Mac, and Linux.
  • Payment Flexibility: Supports over 100 payment methods across 35+ currencies.

Steam is largely considered the dominant force in PC gaming, with many developers seeing it as a primary platform for reaching a global audience.

  • Digital Storefront: Buy games from a massive catalog ranging from AAA titles to indie games, often featuring exclusive sales and discounts.
  • Library & Management: Organizes installed games, handles updates, and provides cloud saves.
  • Community Hub: Features user reviews, discussion forums, friend lists, in-game chat, and marketplace to trade virtual items.
